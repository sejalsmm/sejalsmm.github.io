Earned Nov 2019

GIRL SCOUT GOLD AWARD

During Sejal's final level of being a Girl Scout, she sought out to earn her gold award afyer completing her silver and broze awards in prior years. She wanted to make an impact on an issue that she cared about, so she decided to lead an engineering summer camp program for young women who otherwise do not have access to STEM education. During the program, she taught elementary and middle school girls the basics of arduino, circuits, and compiled all of this into the creation of an LED matrix. After 80 total hours, Sejal earned her Gold Award and successfully made progress towards narrowing the gender gap in STEM by facilitating girls' interests in engineering.

TECHGIRLS

As Sejal's goal of abridging the gender gap in STEM continued, Sejal involved herself in a local startup where she mentored over 100 girls of varying grade levels in the Web Development and Social Entrepreneurship program.
